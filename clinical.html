    • General Course Information 

      CLINIC's Comprehensive Overview of Immigration Law is a six-week e-learning course designed to help new advocates understand the fundamentals of immigration law and how the different aspects of immigration law come together.  The course presents an overview of core immigration law information and concepts, including what immigration law is about, who enforces immigration law, and the different types of legal status available to non-citizens. It then takes a closer look at specific forms of immigration benefits, and how eligibility is impacted by inadmissibility and deportability issues.  The course also introduces participants to the critical practice skills necessary to be an effective advocate, including interviewing, legal research and professional responsibility rules and best practices.

    • Week One: Introductory Themes: What Does it Mean to Practice Law? Introduction to Legal Skills

      Unit One

      Unit One includes an introduction to the course and to the skills and credentials needed to practice immigration law. We will discuss introductory information about immigration law, eligibility for agency recognition and accreditation, and case management concepts. Your work for this unit includes: (a) reviewing the course syllabus and FAQs to familiarize yourself with how the course works and what work we'll be doing; (b) using the link below to introduce yourself to your fellow course participants; (c) reading the Introduction and Chapter 10 of the Manual; (d) completing the unit one quiz; and (e) solving the Ten-Minute Challenge.  Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.

    • Week Two: Inadmissibility and Deportability Concepts; Review of Selected Grounds of Inadmissibility

      • Unit Three

        In this unit we will start by distinguishing the grounds of inadmissibility from the grounds of deportability. Then we will cover four important grounds of inadmissibility: health-related, fraud and misrepresentation, false claims of U.S. citizenship, and smuggling.

        Your work for this week includes (a) reading Manual Chapter 3 pages 39-45 (through document fraud); (b) reading USCIS Policy Manual Volume 8, Part B on health-based inadmissibility and Parts J and K on fraud, misrepresentation and false claims using the link in the Additional Reading folder; (c) reviewing the Department of State inadmissibility chart in the Additional Reading folder and calculating how many ways you can be inadmissible under INA Sec. 212(a); (d) solving the Ten-Minute Challenge; and (e) taking the unit three quiz.  Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.  

        The unit concludes with a webinar on Monday, April 15 at 2:00 Eastern.

      • Unit Four

        In this unit we will continue our coverage of the most common grounds of inadmissibility. We will cover the crime-related grounds, which include crimes involving moral turpitude, drug offenses, and multiple criminal convictions. We will also cover immigration violations, which include orders of removal, unlawful presence, and the "permanent" bar for an immigration violation followed by a departure and illegal reentry. Your work for this unit includes (a) reading Manual Chapter 3 sections 5-7 (pages 45-52); (b) reviewing INA Sec. 212(a)(9)(A) - (C) and the chart on 212(a)(9) inadmissibility in the Additional Reading folder; (c) completing the exercise on unlawful presence and the permanent bar; (d) solving the Ten-Minute Challenge; and (e) taking the unit four quiz. Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.

        The unit concludes with a webinar on Thursday, April 18 at 2 p.m. Eastern.

    • Week Three: Introduction to Family-Based Immigration

      • Unit Five

        In Unit Five, we will learn about who can immigrate through a family relationship.  We will find out which family members qualify as immediate relatives and what that designation means, and which family members are in the "preference categories" that are subject to annual quotas and longer waits to immigrate.  We will also learn about derivative beneficiaries and how changes in the life of the petitioner and beneficiary impact on eligibility to immigrate, and how long it takes.   Your work for this unit includes (a) reading pages 10 to 17, (Up to Section C )  of Ch. 1 of the manual; (b) reading the I-130 instructions and Visa Bulletin in the Additional Reading folder; (c) reviewing the sample I-130 application and completing the I-130 activity sheet; (d) solving the Ten-Minute Challenge; and (e) taking the unit five quiz.  Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.  

        Unit Five concludes with a webinar on Monday, April 22 at 2:00 p.m. Eastern.

        In Unit Six, we will take a closer look at other issues affecting family-based immigration, including the Child Status Protection Act (CSPA), immigrating through marriage, and immigrating after the petitioner or the principal beneficiary has died.  Your work for this unit includes (a) reading pages 17( starting with Section C) to the end of Chapter 1 and Chapter 4 of the manual on immigrating through marriage; (b) reading a CLINIC article on remedies for surviving relatives in the Additional Reading folder; (c) completing the exercises on the CSPA; (d) solving the Ten-Minute Challenge; and (e) taking the unit six quiz. Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.

    • Week Four: Applying for Lawful Permanent Residence

      • Unit Seven

        In Unit Seven we will learn who qualifies to apply for lawful permanent residence in the United States through adjustment of status.  We will learn about the requirements for adjustment under INA Sec. 245(a) and 245(i) and how to determine who qualifies under each of these sections.  Your work for this unit includes (a) reading Chapter 2, pages 24 to Section B on page 30 of the manual: (b) reading excerpts from Ch. 23.5 of the Adjudicator's Field Manual in the Additional Reading folder; (c) reviewing the sample application for adjustment of status and completing the activity sheet; (d) solving the Ten-Minute Challenge; and (e) taking the unit seven quiz. Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.  

        Unit Seven concludes with a webinar on Monday, April 29 at 2:00 p.m. Eastern.

Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.

    • Week Five: Removal Proceedings; Other Benefits: Humanitarian Relief

      • Unit Nine  

        In Unit Nine we will learn what happens in immigration court, including the rights and remedies for individuals in removal proceedings.  We will also review eligibility to immigrate through employment, and through the diversity visa lottery.  Your work for this unit includes (a) reading chapters 6 & 8 of the Manual; (b) reviewing the sample Notice to Appear in the Additional Reading folder and completing the related exercise; (c) solving the Ten-Minute Challenge; and (d) taking the unit nine quiz.  Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.

      • Unit Ten

        In Unit Ten we will learn about various forms of humanitarian relief, including self-petitioning under the Violence Against Women Act (VAWA), U visas for victims of crimes, Temporary Protected Status, Asylum, Special Immigrant Juvenile Status and Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA).  Your work for this week includes (a) reading chapters 5 and 7 of the Manual; (b) reading additional materials on remedy assessment in the Additional Reading folder (c); reviewing the short scenarios in the remedy assessment exercise and considering what remedy might apply; (d) solving the Ten-Minute Challenge; and (e) completing the unit ten quiz. Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.

    • Week Six: Citizenship and Naturalization; Ethics; Putting it All Together

      • Unit Eleven

        In Unit Eleven we will learn about who qualifies for "automatic citizenship" through acquisition or derivation, and who may become a U.S. citizen by applying for naturalization.  Your work for this unit includes (a) reading Chapter 9 of the Manual; (b) reviewing the materials in the Additional Reading folder; (c) reviewing a sample N-400 application to identify any problem issues or eligibility concerns; (d) solving the Ten-Minute Challenge; and (e) taking the unit eleven quiz.  Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.

        The unit concludes with a webinar on Monday, May13 26 at 2:00  p.m. Eastern.

      • Unit Twelve

        Congratulations! You are almost at the course finish line. As we conclude the course, we will discuss ethical issues in immigration law practice and review course material from the preceding weeks to reinforce key immigration law concepts and issues. We will also consider what next steps and best practices will help you as you work towards gaining the knowledge and skills necessary to be a strong and capable advocate for your clients. Your work for this unit includes (a)reading the materials in the Reading folder, including the chapter on ethics from the CLINIC Family-Based Immigration Manual; the regulations on grounds for practitioner discipline; and the sample retainer agreement; (b) completing the review exercises, which incorporate various issues covered in the first 11 units of the course; (c) solving the Ten-Minute Challenge; (d) posting your "next steps" in the discussion forum concerning what you plan to do to retain and reinforce the knowledge you have gained from this training, AND responding to at least one other student posting; and (e) taking the unit twelve quiz. Raise any questions you have about the course materials in the "Gotta Question?" section at the top of the page.

        The unit concludes with our final course webinar on Thursday, May 16 at 2:00 p.m. Eastern.
